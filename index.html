Privacy Policy

Applies to the PlugZen Chrome extension. Last updated: June 22, 2026.

p>

Short version

PlugZen does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. The extension runs entirely on your device. There are no accounts, no sign-in, no analytics, no advertising, no third-party trackers, and no network requests for normal operation.

What data is collected

None. PlugZen does not request or transmit any personal information.

What is stored on your device

PlugZen stores the following locally in Chrome's standard extension storage (and via Chrome Sync, when enabled, encrypted under your Google account):

  • Profiles (named sets of extensions).
  • Schedules (rules that enable/disable extensions at chosen times).
  • Tags (colored labels you apply to extensions).
  • Locked-extension settings (extensions marked "always keep enabled").
  • Install-time snapshot used by the Restore feature.
  • Import/export JSON when you back up or move your configuration.
  • General preferences (theme, sort order, notification toggles).

This data never leaves your device through PlugZen. Uninstalling the extension removes all locally stored PlugZen data.

Permissions used

  • management — list your installed extensions and enable/disable them. Core function.
  • storagecode> — stores profiles, schedules, tags, and settings on your device.
  • alarms — fires scheduled rules in the background (MV3 requirement).
  • notifications — informs you when a scheduled rule or bulk action runs.
  • contextMenuscode> — adds the right-click "Disable this page's extensions" action.

PlugZen does not request host permissions and does not access any website's content, cookies, history, or browsing data.

Children

PlugZen is intended for a general audience. No personal information is collected from any user, including children.

Third-party services

None. No advertising SDKs, no analytics SDKs, no crash-reporting SDKs, no remote code, no third-party trackers. All code is bundled in the package you install from the Chrome Web Store.

Sharing content

Import/Export is local: PlugZen generates a JSON file and saves it to your device through Chrome's standard download mechanism. You choose where it goes. Nothing is uploaded to any server.

Data security

Because no data is transmitted, there is no data in transit to protect. On-device data is stored in Chrome's standard extension storage, sandboxed per extension by the browser.

Your choices

Clear all PlugZen data by uninstalling the extension from chrome://extensions. You can also disable specific permissions through Chrome's settings; some features will stop working if their underlying permission is revoked.

Changes to this policy

Updates will be published at this URL with a new "Last updated" date. Material changes will also be reflected in the extension's release notes on the Chrome Web Store.
